Chaos proxy (Latency Poison)

External tool by RootSolve. This page describes what it does and links to the app and docs.

Chaos proxy (Latency Poison) is a proxy that sits in front of your services and injects configurable latency — and optionally failures or other behaviour — into HTTP traffic. You use it to see how your app behaves when responses are delayed or unreliable (“latency poison”).

Typical use:

  • Point your client (or another service) at the proxy instead of the real backend.
  • Configure delay, jitter, or failure rates in the proxy.
  • Observe timeouts, retries, and degradation in your app.
